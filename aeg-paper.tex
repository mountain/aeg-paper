\begin{abstract}
Arithmetic expressions are usually regarded as purely syntactic objects whose intermediate evaluation steps disappear
once a final numerical value is produced. In this paper we develop a geometric framework in which the evaluation
dynamics of expressions itself becomes the primary object of study. The starting point is the observation that the basic
operations of addition and multiplication generate two transverse directions of propagation, whose infinitesimal interplay
gives rise to a flow equation
\[
    \frac{da}{ds}=\mu\cos\theta + a\lambda\sin\theta,
\]
a Hamilton--Jacobi--type relation governing how assignment values evolve along distinguished directions. We show
that this flow admits a natural realization on a hyperbolic surface: in the zeroth kind arithmetic expression space
$\mathfrak{E}_0$, defined on the upper half--plane with a weighted hyperbolic metric, the assignment function
$a=-x/y$ simultaneously satisfies the flow equation and a Laplace eigenvalue equation. Within this space, the
non-commutativity of ``add then multiply'' versus ``multiply then add'' manifests as an \emph{arithmetic torsion}
which corresponds exactly to hyperbolic area enclosed by evaluation paths.

To analyse global torsion for arbitrary expression paths, we introduce the \emph{Accumulative Commutative Space
(ACS)}, a commutative parameter plane that records total additive and multiplicative charges. In the ACS a path and
its reversed sequence enclose a well-defined region, and we establish a Stokes--type triple identity expressing global
arithmetic torsion equivalently as an algebraic evaluation difference, an $e^M$-weighted area integral, and a boundary
integral along the enclosing curve.

We then construct a three-dimensional \emph{arithmetic expression contact manifold}, with contact form
$\alpha = da - (\mu\,du + \lambda a\,dv)$, whose horizontal distribution encodes the local non-commutative
dynamics. Legendrian curves with respect to this structure satisfy the arithmetic flow equation, and the associated Lie
algebra is identified as a solvable central extension of $\mathfrak{aff}(1)$. On this manifold we develop an expression
differential calculus $\delta$, compatible with the de Rham differential and exhibiting a curvature proportional to
$\mu\lambda\,du\wedge dv$. Finally, we introduce \emph{arithmetic holomorphic functions}, defined via Cauchy--Riemann
type equations on the contact structure, providing a first glimpse of an emerging geometric function theory for
arithmetic expressions.

Taken together, these constructions offer a unified view of arithmetic evaluation as a geometric flow, echoing themes
from geometric mechanics and contact geometry, and establishing arithmetic expressions as geometric objects equipped
with intrinsic invariants and differential structures.
\end{abstract}
