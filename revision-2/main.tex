\begin{abstract}
We introduce Arithmetic Expression Geometry (AEG), a framework in which arithmetic expressions are studied through the geometry of their evaluation histories. For a distinguished class of threadlike expressions, additive and multiplicative updates determine two generating directions of propagation, and their continuous limit yields an arithmetic flow equation with an \texorpdfstring{$\mathrm{Aff}(1)$}{Aff(1)} Lie-theoretic formulation.

We define an \emph{Arithmetic Expression Space} (AES) as a Riemannian surface equipped with an assignment field satisfying the corresponding first-order law, and we construct basic model spaces \texorpdfstring{$\mathfrak{E}_0$}{E0} and \texorpdfstring{$\mathfrak{E}_1$}{E1}. The first intrinsic invariant of the theory is \emph{arithmetic torsion}, arising from the non-commutativity of additive and multiplicative propagation. To globalize this invariant, we introduce the \emph{Accumulative Commutative Space} (ACS) and prove a Stokes-type triple identity expressing torsion equivalently as an evaluation difference, a weighted area integral, and a boundary integral.

To localize the same phenomenon, we construct a three-dimensional contact manifold with contact form $\alpha = da - (\mu\,du + \lambda a\,dv)$. Its horizontal distribution recovers the propagation directions, yields a natural horizontal differential calculus \texorpdfstring{$\delta$}{delta}, and produces a commutator curvature package proportional to $\mu\lambda\,du\wedge dv$. On this horizontal layer we define \emph{horizontal holomorphic fields} via an arithmetic Cauchy--Riemann system and show that it forces a nontrivial twisted second-order equation. Taken together, these results provide a theorem-driven foundational layer of AEG and clarify the normalization and invariance properties of its core structures.
\end{abstract}
